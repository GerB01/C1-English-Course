Module 01

Overview & Roadmap

Understand precisely what separates a B2 learner from a C1 candidate — and map out the path between them.

The B2 → C1 Gap

At B2, you can communicate with reasonable fluency on familiar topics. At C1, you handle complex, abstract arguments with precision, subtlety, and a naturally varied range of language. The gap is not about knowing more words — it is about using language with greater control.

DimensionB2 (IELTS 5.5–6.5)C1 (IELTS 7.0–8.0)
GrammarMostly accurate simple & compound sentencesWide range of complex structures with flexibility
VocabularyEveryday + some topic wordsPrecise, idiomatic, register-aware
CohesionBasic linkers (and, but, however)Subtle discourse markers & referencing
FluencySome hesitation, rephrasingSmooth, spontaneous, nuanced

IELTS Band Descriptor Snapshot

6.5
Competent user. Generally effective command despite some inaccuracies. Can handle complex language, particularly in familiar situations.
7.0
Good user. Operational command with occasional inaccuracies. Handles complex language well and understands detailed reasoning.
8.0
Very good user. Fully operational command with only occasional unsystematic inaccuracies. Handles complex detailed argumentation well.

Module 02

Advanced Grammar

Grammatical range is the single fastest way to lift a score from 6.5 to 7.5. Focus on control, not complexity for its own sake.

1. Mixed Conditionals

Mixed conditionals combine different time frames — typically a past condition with a present consequence, or vice versa. They are powerful for hypothetical reasoning in Writing Task 2 and Speaking Part 3.

Past conditionif + had + V3
Present resultwould + V
Future effectwould be V-ing

Example

"If governments had invested in renewable energy earlier, we would not be facing such severe climate consequences today."

Why this scores higher ▶

This sentence demonstrates a mixed third-to-second conditional, precise lexical choice (severe consequences), and clear cause–effect reasoning — markers of band 7+.

Exercise 1 — Fill the blank: If she ____ (study) medicine, she would be working in a hospital now.

2. Inversion for Emphasis

Inversion places an adverbial at the front of a sentence and reverses subject–verb order. It is highly characteristic of C1 writing and speaking.

Standard: I have rarely seen such dedication.

Inverted: Rarely have I seen such dedication.

Common triggers

Rarely · Seldom · Not only · Never before · Under no circumstances · Only by · Hardly had

Multiple choice — Which sentence uses inversion correctly?
Not only she speaks English, but also French.
Not only does she speak English, but also French.
Not only speaks she English, but also French.
Error correction — Rewrite correctly: "Hardly I had finished the test when the bell rang."

3. Advanced Passive Structures

Passive voice is essential for academic and formal registers. C1 users go beyond be + V3 to use passive infinitives, passive reporting structures, and causatives.

StructureExample
Reporting passiveIt is widely believed that climate change is accelerating.
Subject passiveClimate change is believed to be accelerating.
CausativeThe committee had the results verified independently.
Transform to passive: "Experts estimate that the project will cost $2 million."

Capstone — Grammar Integration

Rewrite using inversion and a mixed conditional: "I have never regretted a decision more. If I had accepted the offer, I would be in London now."

Mastery Checkpoint

  • I can form and use mixed conditionals accurately
  • I can apply inversion with negative adverbials
  • I can use advanced passive reporting structures
  • I can identify and correct common B2 grammar errors
Module 03

Vocabulary & Collocation

A C1 speaker is not someone who knows rare words — it is someone who uses common words with uncommon precision.

The Register Ladder

Register refers to the level of formality. IELTS examiners reward appropriate register, not the most formal one. Compare how a single idea can be expressed across registers:

Annotated sample opening (Band 7.5)

It is often argued that the widespread use of technology in classrooms has transformed modern education. While this shift has undeniably brought benefits, I would contend that its drawbacks merit equally serious consideration.

Notice: hedged opening, balanced thesis, formal register, no personal pronoun "I think".

Capstone — Mini Essay Planning

Prompt: "Some believe universities should only teach employable skills. To what extent do you agree?" — See model plan ▶

Thesis: While employability is a legitimate concern, universities must preserve their broader intellectual mission.

Body 1: Acknowledge — graduates need marketable skills; rising tuition demands ROI.

Body 2: Counter — critical thinking, research, and civic reasoning are equally employable and socially valuable; narrowing curricula risks producing technicians rather than thinkers.

Conclusion: A balanced curriculum that integrates vocational relevance with liberal inquiry serves both graduates and society.

Mastery Checkpoint

  • I use a variety of cohesive devices beyond "and / but / also"
  • I can describe Task 1 trends with precise academic vocabulary
  • I maintain formal register throughout my writing
Module 05

IELTS Reading

Speed, inference, and paraphrase recognition are the three pillars of a high reading score.

The Three-Pass Method

Pass 1Skim (2 min) — gist & topic sentences
→
Pass 2Scan — locate keywords
→
Pass 3Infer — read around the answer
Paraphrase recognition — "Scientists have long maintained that sleep plays a vital role in memory." Which option paraphrases this?
Sleep is not important for remembering things.
Researchers have consistently argued that sleep is essential for memory formation.
Scientists recently discovered sleep helps memory.

Mastery Checkpoint

  • I skim for gist before answering any question
  • I recognise paraphrase rather than matching exact words
  • I can distinguish True / False / Not Given reliably
